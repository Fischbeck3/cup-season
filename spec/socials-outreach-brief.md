# Socials & outreach brief — pilot → iOS launch → Dec 31

*Brief for a GROWTH, LAUNCH & SCALE lane session (with Business adjacency).
This session PRODUCES the operating plan, calendars, target lists, and draft
copy — it sends NOTHING. Outreach, posting, and account creation are the
user's hands. Talk-first on strategy calls; drafting is the work.*

## Governing docs — execute, don't reinvent

`spec/gtm-year1.md` is the strategy and it is already decided: ARL (Active
Recurring Leagues) is the North Star; the Final Rule ("does this help another
golf group discover, adopt, and keep playing together?") kills any tactic
that buys installs without groups; organic-first with paid near-zero in H1;
depth over breadth (own Phoenix/Scottsdale buddy leagues first); content is
a documentary of real leagues, 60% community stories / 25% educational / 15%
founder. `spec/brand-bible.md` carries voice + campaign cadences;
`spec/product-vision-v1.0.md` the principles. **This session turns that
strategy into a dated, named, week-by-week operating plan.** Where this
brief and gtm-year1 disagree, gtm-year1 wins; flag the conflict.

## Goals (tiered; ARL leads, installs follow — per canon)

The user's stated stretch: 5,000 downloads + 1,000 active users by Dec 31.
Reframed through the GTM's own math (a league = 8–16 golfers arriving
together; installs without leagues churn):

| Tier | Active leagues (ARL-ish) | Weekly-active golfers | Installs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Target** (plan to hit) | 35 | ~400 | ~2,000 |
| **Stretch** (everything works) | 60 | ~700 | ~3,500 |
| **Moonshot** (the user's number) | ~100 | 1,000 | 5,000 |

The moonshot needs a national hit (a r/golf front-pager, a newsletter/podcast
feature) landing on top of a working referral loop — plan the target, build
the surface area for the moonshot. Report weekly on leagues created,
roster-lock rate, and artifact→claim→signup conversions (gtm-year1 §1);
installs get tracked, never optimized.

## Deliverables (the session's output, in order)

1. **Week-by-week operating calendar, now → Dec 31**, in four phases:
   - *Pre-iOS (now → mid-Aug):* channel setup, PIGL-diary content bank,
     App Store listing assets, outreach list warm-up, founding-league
     recruiting quietly open.
   - *iOS launch window (mid-Aug → early Sept):* launch-day runbook, the
     founder story drop (r/golf + newsletters), white-glove surge.
   - *Fall push (Sept → Nov):* Presidents-Cup-week Ryder-mode campaign,
     Phoenix-season course/association outreach, ambassador seeds, Majors
     launch beat (coordinate with the Majors arc — `spec/majors-arc.md`).
   - *Wrapped (Dec):* season recaps, New Year "start your season" wave, the
     Wrapped drop AS the Dec-31 close.
   Each week: one primary move + content beats, owner (founder), and the
   metric it feeds. Respect gtm-year1 §12 — the founder has ~10 hrs/week;
   white-glove Pro onboarding outranks posting volume.
2. **Channel setup checklist + templates.** Which handles to claim
   (IG/TikTok/X/Reddit posture/YouTube parked), bios, link strategy →
   cupseason.app. Content templates built FROM the product's artifacts
   (settlement cards, recaps, standings lines — `brand/` has the marks);
   founder-voice guidelines: authenticity over polish (gtm-year1 §15),
   participate-don't-advertise on Reddit.
3. **Named outreach lists with contact paths + draft first-touch copy**
   (emails/DMs in the founder's voice, ready for the user to personalize
   and send):
   - *Local (Phoenix/Scottsdale/Tempe):* municipal + daily-fee courses and
     their men's/women's club boards (Papago-tier munis first), city rec
     golf programs, the state golf association, college club golf (ASU),
     local golf media/IG accounts, pro shops for flyer/QR placement.
   - *National:* niche golf newsletters and mid-tier podcasts
     (organizer-heavy audiences per gtm-year1 §7 — Fried-Egg-adjacent tier,
     not big media), r/golf and league-adjacent subreddits, golf forums.
   - **Ranked by impact ÷ effort; NO equipment brands, PGA, or big media in
     Y1** (§7 says they flatter the ego and starve the funnel).
   - The **Founding League offer** (first ~5–10 leagues free forever) IS the
     outreach hook — decided program, usable in DMs/calls/flyers. It stays
     off the app's front door (that decision is made) and off any public
     pricing talk.
4. **App Store launch kit:** listing copy (name/subtitle/description/
   keywords), screenshot shot-list, a one-page press kit, launch-day
   runbook. Store copy carries the D39 pot posture VERBATIM in spirit:
   *the app keeps the ledger — money moves between friends, not through the
   app.* Never "betting," never "gambling," never "wager."
5. **Measurement plan** mapped to gtm-year1 §1's leading indicators, and a
   handoff note listing what needs ENGINEERING to instrument (artifact→
   claim→signup attribution is experiment #1 in the ICE backlog) — written
   as a task for the eng lanes, not built in this session.

## Canon guardrails (hard lines for every word drafted)

- **No paid acquisition** plans in this window (warm-retargeting tests are a
  Q3+ decision that isn't this session's to make).
- **No public pricing** — pricing is PARKED; the Founding League offer is
  the only money-adjacent public statement allowed.
- **Pot language:** ledger-only posture, exactly as above. This is also an
  App Store review posture (gtm-year1 §13.5 + §14.4) — treat it as legal
  copy, not marketing copy.
- **"The Pro,"** never commissioner. **"Your number,"** never an official-
  handicap claim (we never resell or impersonate GHIN/WHS).
- Nothing sends, posts, or publishes from the session. Drafts only; the
  user is the publisher.
