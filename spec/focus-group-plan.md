## The strategic frame (read first)

The whole question sits on one tension between two Level-1 principles, and the
study exists to find out which side governs:

- **Golf First + Low Friction** say *don't tax the individual golfer* → charge
  one payer, the Pro, out of the pot. (The working model.)
- But charging the Pro puts **one gatekeeper** between you and a whole league
  converting. If the Pro balks, 12 golfers don't convert — even if all 12 would
  happily chip in $6.

The single most important thing to learn is **not "what's the price"** — it's
**"where does the money actually move, and who says no."**

Two traps this design guards against:
1. **PIGL will lie to you.** Friends + Founding League (free forever) + wanting
   to please the owner = worthless willingness-to-pay signal. PIGL's value is
   *behavioral* (pot size, organizing pain, renewal instinct), not *pricing*.
2. **"Would you pay $X?" gets a useless answer.** Uses price-sensitivity ranges
   and revealed behavior instead of hypotheticals.

## WHO to ask — six seats, ~10–14 people

The buyer is Persona 1 (the Commissioner / "the Pro"). Most of that seat must be
**arms-length** — not close friends.

| # | Seat | Who | Why they're here | Target n |
|---|------|-----|------------------|----------|
| 1 | **PIGL — the Pro's chair** | You + any PIGL member who has run *another* trip/league | Behavioral read on pot economics from the payer's seat. Insider — weight low on price. | 1–2 |
| 2 | **PIGL — rank & file** | 2–3 ordinary PIGL members | Would a $5–8/player line item in the buy-in even register? What IS the pot? | 2–3 |
| 3 | **External Pros** ⭐ | 3–5 people who run *other* leagues/skins/season groups, **not close friends** | The real willingness-to-pay signal. This seat carries the price decision. | 3–5 |
| 4 | **The Trip Guy** | 1–2 who run the annual buddies trip | Episodic, big one-time pot. Tests whether "season pass" framing fits — and whether the Ryder is its own SKU. | 1–2 |
| 5 | **Weekend Golfer** (individual) | 2–3 competitive regulars | Tests the "free forever" hook and whether anyone would *ever* pay individually if the Pro won't. | 2–3 |
| 6 | **The skeptic** | 1 organizer who tried a spreadsheet/other app and **quit** | Tells you what kills adoption before price is on the table. | 1 |

**Recruiting reality:** Seat 3 makes or breaks the study. Confirmed reachable
(2026-07-16) — Jerecho can find 3–5 arms-length Pros. Without them the study can
only validate PIGL's happiness, not price.

**Format:** 1:1 or pairs, 20–30 min, live (call or in-person). **Not** a group
room — golfers anchor to each other and you lose independent price reads. Seats 2
and 5 may be a short async survey; seats 1, 3, 4, 6 should be live.

## THE QUESTIONS

### Block A — Anchor to the pot, before the app is mentioned (any league-runner)

1. "Walk me through last season's money. What did everyone put in, who collected
   it, where did it sit?" *(pot size + treasurer)*
2. "What did organizing it actually cost you — dollars *and* hours? Venmo
   chasing, a spreadsheet, a paid app?" *(current spend + pain = the value you replace)*
3. "Did anyone in your group pay for anything digital last year — an app,
   scoring service, GPS thing? What happened?" *(revealed preference)*

### Block B — Season-pass price point (Van Westendorp, as % of pot)

Show the product working (guest-claim link, settlement card, standings). Frame it
as *"the whole league, one season, paid once out of the pot":*

4. At what price is this **so cheap** you'd question whether it's any good?
5. At what price is it a **bargain** — clearly worth it, easy yes?
6. At what price does it start feeling **expensive** — you'd have to think?
7. At what price is it **too expensive** — you wouldn't do it, period?

Gatekeeper test — the most important pair in the study:

8. **(Pro)** "If the app cost $60 for the season, who decides yes/no, and does it
   come out of the pot or your pocket?"
9. **(Members, separately)** "If your Pro said 'the app's $6 each this season' —
   instant yes, grumble, or no?"

> The **gap between 8 and 9 is your conversion risk.** If members would chip in
> but the Pro won't front/justify it, the problem isn't price — it's making the
> pot-deduction invisible and automatic.

### Block C — Season-1-free scope (test the renewal cliff)

10. "If your first season were free, and season 2 cost $60 from the pot — what
    happens at renewal? Re-up without blinking, or does 'wait, it was free'
    become a fight?"
11. "Does a free first season make you *more* likely to commit a real league — or
    just train everyone to expect free?"

> Probing one danger: **free-season-1 as a switching moment vs. as
> habituation.** If renewal triggers a fight → longer free runway for Founding
> Leagues only + paid-from-day-one for everyone after, so nobody learns the "it
> was free" story.

### Block D — Is the Ryder ever paid? (à la carte test)

Show the Ryder (standalone event). To the Trip Guy especially:

12. "You run a one-weekend Ryder for the boys. Rather it cost ~$20 for that
    event — or be free but only inside a $60 season?"
13. "Is a one-weekend event something you'd pay for *on its own*, separate from
    any season?"

> The Ryder maps to **episodic play** — a natural standalone SKU *and* a natural
> free viral funnel (guest-claim → foursome-by-foursome). Block D says which:
> **paid add-on** (Trip Guy revenue) or **free hook** that seeds conversions.

### Block E — The kill questions (skeptic + everyone)

14. "What would make you *not* use this even if it were free?"
15. "Last app your group tried and dropped — what killed it?"

## Pressure-test: Pro-pays-from-pot vs. alternatives

| Model | For | Against | What resolves it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Per-league season pass, Pro pays from pot** *(working model)* | One sale to the person already collecting money. Honors Golf First + "I'll run the league." Priced against the pot. | Single gatekeeper can block a league. Small pots make $49–99 a real ask. Free tier may be too sticky to convert. | Block B q8–9 gap; Block A pot size |
| **B. Per-player subscription** | More payers = resilient revenue; each golfer self-selects. | High friction; violates Low Friction + Golf First; the TheGrint tax you rejected. | Block C q9 |
| **C. Freemium + paid league features** | Free identity hook stays; monetize power-organizer features. | Risks gutting the season — the season IS the product, not a feature. | Block E |
| **D. Free now, monetize later (B2B/sponsor)** | Zero friction; grow first. | No revenue path proven; defers the answer, not just the invoice. | Whether seat 3 shows any willingness at all |

**Working read:** A is right, but its failure mode is the **gatekeeper, not the
price.** Likely fix (if data supports): make the pot-deduction *invisible* — the
pass is a pre-filled line item in the buy-in the Pro already collects, so no
golfer writes a separate check and the Pro never fronts real money. That's a
**mechanics-level** consequence needing a `decision-log.md` entry when built.

## Founding League rollout

Decided: PIGL + first ~5–10 leagues **free forever**. To make it a growth asset,
not just a discount:

1. **Status, not a coupon.** Visible **Founding League** badge, permanent, plus
   soft founder perks: early access to new modes, name in an in-app founders
   list, a direct line to the roadmap. Free-forever is the floor; *status* is
   the point.
2. **Selection criteria** (the 5–10 are your permanent panel): a *real* league
   playing a *full season*; a Pro who'll give feedback; archetype + geographic
   diversity (one club league, one weekend group, one trip crew, spread beyond
   Tempe — don't clone PIGL).
3. **Sequence:** PIGL → warm network (2–3) → **referrals from those** (stronger,
   self-selected, tests the growth loop live).
4. **Close it loudly.** Announce the cap (~8–10) and shut it when full. Scarcity
   turns free-forever into a club you missed. "Founding League #7 of 10" is an
   artifact.
5. **Soft obligation:** post rounds, run a season, answer a few questions
   monthly. Free forever in exchange for being the panel. Say it out loud.

> The efficient move: **focus group and Founding League seeding are the same
> motion.** Every arms-length Pro in seat 3 who lights up is a candidate.

## Level-1 posture (open — being resolved 2026-07-16)

At $49–99/league/season the math is a **community/sustainability** business, not
venture-scale. That's a *posture*, not a problem: optimize Block B for **"a price
leagues renew without resentment,"** not **"the max we can extract."**

Three Level-1 commitments already imply the sustainability posture:
- **Golf First** — aggressive pricing pushes cost onto the individual golfer
  (the door killed twice: Verified tier dead; never resell the Index).
- **Pot tracked, never held** — revenue is structurally capped at a painless
  slice of a pot you don't touch.
- **No paid acquisition** — growth is goodwill; resentment isn't lost revenue,
  it's lost *distribution*.

**Legitimate thing to hold open:** a later **Pro *layer*** for power-organizers
(draft engines, advanced Ryder, multi-league dashboards) — a second SKU bought
willingly, base stays painless. That's an **upsell**, not extraction, and it
leaves Block B untouched.

**Open call:** is the held-open path (a) the upsell layer [recommended], or (b)
genuinely pricing the *base pass* to extract? If (b), Block B needs
value-anchoring questions added, and it reopens the TheGrint door.
