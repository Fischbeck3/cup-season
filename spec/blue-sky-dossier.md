# Blue-sky dossier — 2026-07-21

*Output of the blue-sky ideation session (`spec/blue-sky-brief.md`). Ideas,
not decisions: nothing here is committed, tasked, or logged. Entries are
ranked by the Cup Season Test — "if this disappeared tomorrow, would golfers
miss it because it made their golf life richer?" — never by buildability.
Horizon tags: **adjacent** (rides machinery that exists) · **far** (needs a
new arc) · **moonshot** (needs a different company year). Every entry names
what it serves, what it displaces or costs, and any CONFLICT with canon —
per the hierarchy-of-truth rule, conflicts are named, never silent. The full
raw divergence list is Appendix A; nothing was thrown away.*

Canon lenses used: `product-vision-v1.0.md` (five principles, the Test),
the 2030 prospectus (Crew/Cup/Rivalry/Record, the one-more-round rule),
`gameplay-modes-working.md` (parked: §8→D51, §9 earned markers, §10.9 annual
Major, §11 audit parks), `decision-log.md` D1–D55, `gtm-year1.md` §3.

## The sixteen

### 1. The Belt

One continuous champion object per crew — not minted by a cup, but **held**.
The Belt passes hands the moment someone beats the holder head-to-head in any
counting context: outscore them on vs-your-number in a shared round, take a
duel, win a match. Its provenance log is the point — "held 41 days by Ed ·
taken at Papago · 9 reigns since 2026" — and it rides the holder's golfer
card and the crew room until it's taken. Every shared Saturday with the
holder is automatically a title fight, which means the Belt generates stakes
*between* cups: mid-season lulls, the off-season, the random Tuesday nine.
Boxing's lineal-champion model, which golf's buddy culture already speaks
("you can't retire the belt from the couch").

- **Serves:** Rivalry + Crew + Feels Alive; the one-more-round rule made
  standing — the only way to take it is to show up.
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing built; fills the dead air between cups. Cost
  is a second honors system to keep legible beside trophies.
- **CONFLICT:** prospectus law — *trophies are minted only by cups, never
  given, always won*. The Belt is won but never minted and never finished, so
  it must be a new object class (a title, regalia), not a trophy: it engraves
  nothing, sits in no case, and must never dilute trophy scarcity.
- **Horizon:** adjacent (duel machinery, shared-round facts, and the faceted
  rivalry object all exist).

### 2. Last Round With

The Record already knows the last round you played with every person who's
shared a card with you — surface it. "You and Cole — last round 14 months
ago," one tap to a gathering with both of you staged. A yearly Record chapter
lists who you played most and who drifted; the reunion prompt whispers, never
guilts. This is the prospectus coda (the June 2027 round) turned proactive:
the app that keeps your Saturdays should occasionally hand one back before
it's too late. The one-more-round rule pointed at lapsed friendships instead
of rivals.

- **Serves:** the Record + Memory is sacred; arguably the purest expression
  of the Final Rule in the corpus.
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing — rides the played-with facts already
  auto-captured (gameplay-modes item 15). Cost is tonal: melancholy handled
  badly reads as guilt.
- **CONFLICT:** none. D23 applies (the nudge names its emotion — this one is
  *longing*, and it must never fire on a schedule, only on a threshold).
- **Horizon:** adjacent.

### 3. The Forfeit Ledger

Stakes beyond money, tracked with the same rigor as the pot. Stake templates
where the currency is golf-native or social: loser hosts the cookout, winner
picks the next course, strokes-next-time, the captaincy, standing bounties
("first ace: steak dinner from everyone — open since 2026"). Each forfeit is
named, dated, settled with a tap, and archives into the rivalry facets like
any other meeting ("the Lawn Bet: Ed 4–3"). A $0 league stops being the
poor cousin of a money league — it's a league whose currency is pride with
receipts. Twenty years on, the Wager Archive is lore no spreadsheet ever
kept.

- **Serves:** the Stake object + D39's exact posture (the ledger, never the
  bank) extended past dollars; Memory > Statistics — stakes become stories.
- **Displaces / costs:** the money-only imagination of the pot. Brushes D21
  (the Callout settles a *number*; forfeits give any settled thing a
  *consequence*) — compose them, don't fork.
- **CONFLICT:** none — this deepens D39 rather than bending it.
- **Horizon:** adjacent.

### 4. The Shoebox

The scan pipeline (D36 — vision model, structured outputs, credits, kill
switch) pointed backward in time: the shoebox of old scorecards becomes
**archive rounds**, and the Record reaches back before the app existed —
"1,204 rounds since 1994." Archive rounds are a quarantined class: photo
receipt attached, no index effect, no season eligibility, no attestation
claim — pure memory. This is the prospectus year-5 "memory vault" pulled
forward because the hard part (reading a scorecard with a model) already
shipped. The moat compounds retroactively: a 55-year-old's entire golf life
moves in on day one, and no competitor can copy a past they never held.

- **Serves:** the Record — the forty-year product; Memory is sacred.
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing live. Costs scan spend (D36's credit
  machinery already governs it) and a legibility burden: archive vs live must
  be unmistakable everywhere.
- **CONFLICT:** §16 receipts / handicap integrity if archive rounds ever
  leak into scoring or the index. The quarantine class **is** the design —
  named here so nobody softens it later for engagement's sake.
- **Horizon:** far (tech adjacent, product far).

### 5. In Memoriam

When a crew member dies, their profile becomes a memorial: rounds immutable
forever (already true by construction), marker retired within the crew,
trophies engraved permanently, Record visitable. The crew can raise an
annual Memorial in their name on the annual-Major lineage machinery (§10.9)
— "playing the Frank Memorial" becomes how the crew keeps playing with
Frank. The prospectus stakes the company's existence on a February-night
screen that includes a father's last round; this is that promise kept at its
hardest moment, and the moment every long-lived crew will eventually face.

- **Serves:** Memory is sacred / "custodians of people's Saturdays" — the
  literal test of the sentence.
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing. Cost is that sacred ground tolerates no
  half-build: verification, family wishes, and estate control are
  policy questions that must be answered before any pixel.
- **CONFLICT:** none with canon. With reality: policy-first, build-second.
- **Horizon:** far (small build, heavy care).

### 6. The Gallery

A crew's live day, watchable from the couch. Non-playing members (and
invited family/guests) get a live view — hole chips landing, the match pill,
the Wolf banner — and a **roar** button. Roars batch and land on the course
phone only between holes: "7 roars waiting" at the turn. The real-major
couch behavior (leaderboard open, group chat lit) pointed at golf you
actually care about — your crew's. Spectator links double as guest-claim
surfaces for whoever's watching without an account.

- **Serves:** Feels Alive + the five behaviors (open on rest days) + the
  gathering owning its audience, not just its players.
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing — rides live rounds, realtime, and push
  rails that exist. Cost is live-day discipline (battery, attention).
- **CONFLICT:** the pocket principle — the phone spends 4 hours in a pocket
  and 40 seconds in a hand. Roars must never buzz mid-hole; between-holes
  batching is load-bearing, and D23's fence governs every notification here.
- **Horizon:** adjacent-far (rails exist; the restraint is the work).

### 7. The Summit

Two crews, one weekend. Minimal form: the champions duel — Points King vs
Points King, best card of the weekend. Full form: an inter-crew Ryder on the
event rails, city-derby lineage attached ("PIGL v. the Mesa boys — 3rd
annual"). Winner's crew holds a shared trophy; both boards carry the story;
the losing crew books the rematch. This is spec §17's "two leagues ask to
play each other" door — designed before anyone knocks, so the answer is a
weekend, not a roadmap item.

- **Serves:** Crew (crews become characters in a bigger story) + the growth
  loop (the other crew must onboard to answer the challenge).
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing; events schema + duels carry the minimal
  form nearly as-is.
- **CONFLICT:** §17 explicitly *waits for pull* — clubs/B2B deferred until
  two real leagues ask. Timing conflict, not principle conflict: this entry
  is the ready answer, never the build trigger.
- **Horizon:** far.

### 8. The Gauntlet

One month, one course — the course is the opponent. Every member round at
the named course counts; the board is best vs-your-number *at the house*;
the course page grows teeth ("Papago owns Ed: 0-for-11 against his
number"). Courses stop being dropdown rows and start accruing grudges,
which is exactly what course-as-character means competitively. Between-cup
texture with zero new currency — it's a filter over rounds wearing a story.

- **Serves:** Course as a character (prospectus object) + season texture +
  Real Golf (nothing new to track, just somewhere to point the month).
- **Displaces / costs:** a quiet month. Cost: geography bias — the course's
  regulars start warm; the vs-your-number frame absorbs most of it.
- **CONFLICT:** none — a clean lens over rounds, the canonical shape.
- **Horizon:** adjacent.

### 9. The Sunday Paper & the Steward

The crew's week composes into a front page — standings drift, headline
round, photo of the week, quote of the week pulled from the chirp archive —
styled like a sports section, dropped Sunday evening, share-ready. A
rotating **Steward** (opt-in, one line and one pick a week) adds a human
byline over the auto-composed page; the Pro's desk can pin. It gives the
week a close, the crew an appointment, and the 10 members who aren't the
Pro a small honor to hold. Fully auto-composed when nobody stewards — the
ritual never blocks on labor.

- **Serves:** ritual + Memory > Statistics (the week becomes an artifact) +
  GTM §3 (a share object that carries the join path).
- **Displaces / costs:** absorbs the D29 digest into a bigger object rather
  than adding a second one.
- **CONFLICT:** Principle 2 (Low Friction — never require golfer labor). The
  Steward survives only as volunteer regalia with a full automatic fallback;
  the moment it's a chore, it's dead canon.
- **Horizon:** adjacent.

### 10. Marker Provenance

§9 resurrected and sharpened: earned markers aren't unlockables, they're
**medals with papers**. Every earned marker carries its provenance line —
"The Jug · first sub-80 · June 2026 · Papago" — and taps through to the
round that earned it (§16, applied to the avatar itself). Picked markers
remain free identity; earned ones read differently on sight (an engraved
ring), and the Major champion's marker (§10.6) is the natural first mint.
This is the golf-native answer to achievements: no badge grid, no XP — the
thing you already place on the green every Saturday, carrying your history.

- **Serves:** identity + Memory > Statistics + receipts-everywhere.
- **Displaces / costs:** the badge-grid temptation, permanently.
- **CONFLICT:** none — §9 is parked, and the brief invites resurrection.
  Protocol stands: a decision-log entry before any build.
- **Horizon:** adjacent.

### 11. The Almanac

The app knows it's winter. Off-season state: "41 days to the thaw" on Home,
the finished season replayed a chapter a week, next season's time capsule
(sealed predictions, opened at the crowning), bylaw debates on the board,
and the **long winter draft** — squads assembled one ceremonial pick a week
through the dark months (D54's draft-night reveal, stretched to fill
February). The prospectus names February *longing* as a design goal; the
Almanac is longing given a surface. Off-season churn is the quiet killer of
season products — this is the retention answer that isn't a streak flame.

- **Serves:** "the app knows what time it is" + return-every-season + the
  emotional-goals calendar.
- **Displaces / costs:** the dead-app winter. Cost: seasonal code paths; and
  honesty — the Phoenix beachhead barely has a winter, so year-1 value is
  thin (it matters the year the second metro does).
- **CONFLICT:** Feels Alive vs guilt — winter content must ache, never nag.
  No streaks, no re-engagement drip; D23 governs.
- **Horizon:** far.

### 12. The Pilgrimage

A quest cup over geography. The crew names its circuit — "the Phoenix Slam:
10 munis" — and the season is collecting it: play each course, best
vs-your-number at each, a completion bonus for finishing the map. The
standings ARE the map, filling in as the crew travels. Trip-guy native, and
the unplayed course becomes the season's standing nag — one more round, but
*there*. Mechanically nearly free (rounds + course filter); the map is the
product.

- **Serves:** Course + the Golf Trip Guy persona + one-more-round with a
  destination attached.
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing; a new cup format in the gallery.
- **CONFLICT:** brushes "not a GPS/travel app." Survives under the brief's
  own carve-out: the unlock is precisely the crossing of courses × season ×
  crew — no yardages, no bookings, just a map that keeps score.
- **Horizon:** far.

### 13. The Clubhouse Shelf

The digital honors become objects. The season yearbook — photos, final
tables, the archived trash talk, the recap film's stills — printed and
shipped. The jug actually engraved. The Belt (entry 1) physical. Trophies
outliving the phone, literally, on a real shelf at the real 19th hole.
Revenue that never gates play and never touches the golfer's data: memory
as merchandise, priced against the pot like everything else in the canon.

- **Serves:** Memory is sacred (the artifact outlives the device) + the
  monetization canon's spirit (never gate the golfer; artifacts market the
  product).
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing digital. Cost: fulfillment ops, quality
  bar (a bad print of your season is worse than none).
- **CONFLICT:** D39's "never held" governs *stakes* — selling goods makes
  Cup Season a retailer (Stripe unparked, commerce ToS). Name it now so
  ledger canon stays clean: commerce ≠ stakes, and pot money never touches
  the store. Pricing itself is PARKED with the rest of monetization.
- **Horizon:** far.

### 14. The Solstice

One day a year, every crew plays. The longest Saturday in June: one
distributed, handicapped table; **crews roll up as units** — your crew's
card on a national wall, "214 crews played the Solstice." The prospectus
year-10 CupSeason Open, seeded early as an annual ritual while the number
is small enough to feel like a founding. The one day "every round counts
toward something" scales past your crew — toward everything.

- **Serves:** the institution arc + belonging to something larger than the
  crew, one day a year.
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing; costs real infra and event ops, and it's
  meaningless below a critical mass of crews.
- **CONFLICT:** "the crew is the customer" vs a global table. Survives only
  crew-first: the crew's row is the unit, no stranger feed, no individual
  global vanity board. The moment it's an individual leaderboard, it's
  someone else's app.
- **Horizon:** moonshot.

### 15. The Lines

Monday, the app posts the week's lines: the clash favorite, over/unders on
the crew's Saturday scoring, "Ed to beat his number — likely." The crew
picks. Zero money, ever. Picks grade Sunday; backers' records become lore
("Marcus picks 70% against Ed") and trash-talk ammunition with receipts.
The couch gets skin in Saturday without a dollar riding, and Monday gets a
reason to open the app. Included *because* its conflicts are instructive,
not despite them.

- **Serves:** Feels Alive + rivalry fabric + rest-day opens.
- **Displaces / costs:** dead Mondays. Cost: grading needs care in a
  small-crew week (thin slates).
- **CONFLICT — three, all named:** (a) *fantasy scaffolding* — the
  prospectus's first correction says orchestrate real competition, never
  simulate it; picks are commentary on real golf, but the line is thin and
  must stay commentary. (b) *Bookie optics* — the brief auto-rejects
  app-as-bookie; zero-money is load-bearing, and the first dollar attached
  kills the feature, permanently. (c) *Prediction pressure* — being picked
  against is a needle; D23's tone fence governs.
- **Horizon:** far.

### 16. The Amateur Number

Every golfer receives a permanent member number at first posted round —
"Member #241 · since 2026" — stamped on the golfer card, the trophies, the
yearbook spine. Nothing to earn, nothing to buy: institutional identity in
one integer. Low numbers become lore as the institution grows (the
founding-league badge, generalized to persons), and the card starts reading
like something issued rather than something configured.

- **Serves:** identity + the institution arc; near-zero cost, compounds
  daily.
- **Displaces / costs:** nothing.
- **CONFLICT:** none.
- **Horizon:** adjacent.
