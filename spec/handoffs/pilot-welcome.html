A season-long game built on the golf you already play, and here's what it is and how to jump in.

The gist

Every round you post lands on a shared board the whole crew can see, so a good one becomes its own little moment and a blow-up gets you chirped just the same. You're really playing two games at once, since every round earns points for your squad in the team race while it also climbs your own line on the individual board. It all runs off your handicap, so the 20-index guy can hang with the scratch player, and both races keep tightening the deeper into the season you get.

It's basically fantasy golf, except the stats are your own actual rounds instead of made-up teams and players you've never met. It's just you and the guys you already text about tee times.

How you score

After a round you'll see a number like +3.2 vs index, which is how you played against your own handicap (3.2 shots better than expected, in that case). Higher is better, and that number is really the whole currency, so when you play to your potential the points follow.

Getting in · about five minutes

  1. 1

    Open cupseason.app on your phone. If you want it to feel like a real app, hit Share and then Add to Home Screen.

  2. 2

    Sign in with your email. A code lands in your inbox, so there's no password to remember, and once you type it in you're through.

  3. 3

    Set up your golfer card. Add your name, home course, index, and a ball marker that's yours, all of which takes about a minute.

  4. 4

    Add your buddies. Search their name or @handle, and when someone adds you it pops up right on your home screen where you can tap Accept.

  5. 5

    Post a round after you play. Enter your score, search the course, and pick the date, and since the app already knows the rating and slope it scores you and drops it on the board for the crew to see.

Jerecho will get you into the league, so you'll either find an invite waiting in the app or he'll hand you a code to punch in.

A few things to know

  • Pilot

    It won't be perfect yet, so when something's confusing or clunky, tap "Tell us how it's going" at the bottom of the screen. That's the whole point right now, so tell us wherever it trips you up.

  • Free

    There's nothing to pay while we're testing, so just get out and play.

  • The pot

    It's optional and hands-off, so if your group plays for something the app tracks who's in and where it stands without ever touching the money, and you settle up however you always have.

  • On your phone

    It's built for your phone, so you can post a round right from the parking lot.
