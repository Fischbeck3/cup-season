Cup Season · the shape of a season

Eight golfers. Four squads. One cup.

Creation to champion for a 2-per-squad league, on a real calendar: a three-month season, Sunday Aug 2 → Saturday Oct 31, 2026 — thirteen whole weeks, the last four of which are the Cup Final.

2
Week 0 · over a group chat

The crew joins, the draw drops

The invite link lands in the chat; each golfer signs in with an email code and builds their card — name, home course, ball marker, handicap (typed in, or the app derives it from their first three posted scores). When all eight are in, the Pro locks the roster and runs the blind draw: a server-side shuffle, revealed to everyone as a board post. Rigging-proof by construction.

3
Sunday, Aug 2 — the season opens

Post rounds from anywhere; every one scores

No schedule, no tee-time coordination. Anyone plays any course, any day, and posts the score in under a minute — front nine, back nine, done. The engine handicaps it against their own number, so the 6-index and the 24-index are on level terms.

The 12-point ceiling is the anti-sandbag: a padded handicap can only ever buy the top band. The 5-point floor means a posted 98 beats an unposted 82 — there is no round not worth posting.

Every month · Aug & Sep

Best 4 count, the floor keeps everyone honest

A golfer can post ten rounds a month — only the best four feed the squad. A new better round displaces the worst counting one in real time. Here's A1's August:

A1's counting month: 37. A2 adds 29 → Saguaro's August is 66. Displaced rounds stay visible — the cap is machinery you can watch, and every points figure taps open to the rounds behind it.

The turn of the month — automated, on the board

At midnight on the 1st the month closes itself: floor penalties post to a visible ledger with reasons (first miss of the season is auto-forgiven — the bye), the board gets its "AUGUST CLOSED" post, standings snapshot, and handicap indexes revise from recent scores. Nobody keeps the books; the books keep themselves.

Sunday, Oct 4 · four weeks out — the Turn

Seeds lock: two squads make the Final

The season table has done its job. The top two squads advance; the regular-season leader carries a +10 head start into the Final. Everything below the cup line keeps playing — the individual races run to the last day.

"Scored fresh" is the point: 142 vs 131 becomes 10 vs 0. A season of grinding earns position, not safety — the Final is winnable from either chair. Dead heat after four weeks? The ladder: head-to-head months won → best single month → fewest rounds used → a logged coin flip.

The Trophy Room

Forty-eight hours of grace for rounds played on or before the final Saturday, then the season crowns itself. Home becomes the Trophy Room: champion banner, final table, superlatives, and the pot settlement card — screenshot-shaped by design.
