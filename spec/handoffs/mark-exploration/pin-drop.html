The swirl and the pin drop

Four balls spiral into one point; the point becomes the hole; the pin drops hard enough to squash the turf; the swallowtail unfurls; and the four resurface at their corners. The end frame is the lockup - the four-ball with the planted pin. The pin itself is rebuilt: tapered shaft, tournament swallowtail with a shadowed fold, a collar where it meets the turf. Nothing bland about a flag that just landed.

The choreography

2.6s - swirl, vanish, drop, squash, unfurl, resurface
Phase 1 - the swirl (0-1.25s). One carousel rotation, 400 degrees, while each ball pulls its radius to zero: a true inward spiral, pure transforms, --roll family easing.

Phase 2 - the vanish (1.22-1.35s). The four compress into the point they made. The hole opens exactly there - the hole is what the gather leaves behind.

Phase 3 - the drop (1.5-2.4s). The pin falls hard, ease-in like gravity; the turf squashes and recovers; the swallowtail unfurls with an overshoot and one wag; the ground answers with a single ring.

Phase 4 - the return (2.15-2.6s). Three resurface at the open corners; the violet ball stays down. Somebody's always in the cup. Final frame = the lockup.
