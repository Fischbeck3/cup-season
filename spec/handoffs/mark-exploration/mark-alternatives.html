Six marks from outside the canon

Flag and orbit forgotten. Each of these starts from a different thing that is true about Cup Season — the record, the cut, the crew, the vessel, the ritual — and reduces it to geometry. Every one is shown at 128 and at favicon size, because a mark that dies at 24px isn't a mark.

The Tally
product action · the record
Four strokes and the champagne fifth — the count that closes the group. It's how golfers have kept score on paper forever, and the fifth stroke is the season settling. Brutally simple, prints at any size, one-color versions for free.
The Cut Line
construction geometry · the stakes
A ball crossed by the dashed gold line — above it or below it, the whole season in one sentence. The dashed cut line already lives in your standings UI, so the logo and the product would speak the same sentence.
The Four-Ball
negative space · the crew
Four balls in a diamond; the hole is the empty center — the thing everyone's circling is the space between them. Reads as a foursome, a compass, and a target at once. The strongest tiny-size survivor here.
Run It Back
metaphor fusion · the ritual
A season that loops — the circle never quite closes, and the loose end is a champagne pennant. "One more round" as geometry. The renewal verb the whole business model leans on, drawn in one stroke.
The Jug & the Ball
the vessel · the prize
The trophy straight on, a champagne ball sitting in the bowl — the season IS the cup, and what you put in it is rounds. Warmest and most literal of the six; instantly legible to any golfer alive.
The C Planted
monogram + meaning
The C of Cup Season as the bowl of the course itself, with the pin planted dead center rising out the open top. Monogram, vessel, and flagstick in two strokes. The most "2030" of the six — it would still look right on a clubhouse wall in twenty years.

All six hold at favicon size and go one-color for print without losing their idea. None of them retires the current flag-and-orbit — that refinement stands on the other board. Pick a lane (or a crossbreed: the Four-Ball's negative-space hole inside Run It Back's loop is a seventh mark hiding here) and the winner gets the full system pass: grounds, clearspace, wordmark lockup, and the asset regeneration.
