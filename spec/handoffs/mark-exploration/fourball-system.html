The Four-Ball, incorporated

Four balls, one open center. The mark works because the product already speaks it: four squad colors, a crew circling one thing, and a hole nobody owns until the season says so. Below — the two states, the lockups, the icons, the motion, and exactly where it lands in the app.

1 · The two states — season open, cup taken

season open · the default mark
cup taken · champion contexts only
mono ring · print / stamp / etch

The champagne center is EARNED. The everyday mark keeps the hole empty — the season is open, the thing in the middle belongs to nobody. The gold ball drops into the center only where something was won: trophy case, champion cards, season recaps, the Wrapped drop. The logo itself obeys the token charter — gold is never decoration, and the brand carries a story in one dot.

4 · Motion — the mark IS the entry choreography

the gather · four roll in, the ground answers
The gather replaces the splash finale: four balls roll to their corners on the --roll curve, the ring answers from the empty center. Same 2.05s door budget, same reduced-motion law (all four simply present).

The loading state is the four dots orbiting once. The champion moment is the champagne ball dropping into the center — the only time the hole fills, once a season, with the thock it deserves.

5 · Where the product already speaks it

· Squad swatches in the climb and roster are already these four dots — the logo becomes the system they were always quoting.
· Draft night: picks are balls taking their place around the center.
· Wolf: the lone-wolf call is one ball stepping out of the diamond — 1 v 3, the mark itself playing the game.
· The Ryder: two dots vs two dots. The Major: every ball converging on one hole — the crest art already tells this story with tracers.
· Trophy case: won seasons render the cup-taken state; the wall of champions is a wall of filled centers.
