Four-ball, golf-true

Same diamond, no pin. Six ways to make the golf unmistakable — the cup seen from above, the dimples, the tee, the green, the shot arriving. Each at full size and favicon size.

The Cup Rim
The center void gets a rim — the hole seen from above. Four balls around the cup; the game named in one thin ring. The smallest possible change that makes it golf.
The Cup, Cut
The center is a dark cut hole with a lip highlight — depth, not void. Reads "sunk" instantly. At favicon size the highlight drops and the dark cup carries it.
Dimpled + Rim
Dimples at display sizes, plain at small — the balls are unmistakably golf balls up close, and the mark self-simplifies below 48px. One rule, both worlds.
Next on the Tee
The top ball sits on a tee — one of the four is always up next. Asymmetry gives it a story ("whose honors?") and the tee is the one golf object nobody else's logo uses.
The Green
A soft irregular contour around the diamond — the putting green from above, four balls on the dance floor. The contour drops at small sizes; the aerial-green reading stays in people's heads.
Shots Landing
Each ball trails the last few feet of its flight — four shots arriving from four directions, your crest's tracer language distilled. Tails drop at small sizes. The most kinetic static mark here.

All six keep the diamond and the earned-center rule (champagne ball drops in the cup only where something was won). The rim or cut center can ride ANY of the other treatments — dimples, tee, green, tracers are composable layers on the same chassis.
