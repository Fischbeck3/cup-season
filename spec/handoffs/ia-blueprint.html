Cup SeasonIA Blueprint · v1 · July 2026

Four questions.
Four rooms.
One atom.

A ground-up information architecture for an app that is a stats tracker, a fantasy league, a social feed, and a tee sheet at once — without feeling like four apps stapled together.

00Diagnosis — why it feels cluttered

Cup Season grew a screen per system. Leagues got a hub, stats got a page, friends got a tab, the calendar got a tab, the pot got a screen, posting got a page and a card on Home. Each was reasonable alone. Together they mean most features have two or three homes, and the user is never sure which one is canonical. The pilot said this eleven different ways: "redundant," "two home tabs," "should be separate from settings," "not accessible without a league."

Here is the irony: the database already solved this. Migration 007 made the round a global, immutable fact that belongs to a golfer, and made every league a lens that scores it through its own bylaws. Post once, appear everywhere. The schema is elegant. The UI never adopted the same idea — it still treats every surface as a silo that needs its own copy of everything. This blueprint is the client-side 007.

The unlock: screens are lenses, not silos. A round is posted once and read through many lenses (your card, your feed, each league's standings). A friend is added once and appears in every picker. The moment a feature needs a second home, it isn't a place — it's a verb or a lens.

01Philosophy

1 · The round is the atom

Strava feels effortless because everything radiates from one object: the activity. Cup Season's activity is the round. Every screen in this architecture is a view over rounds: the feed is rounds as stories, standings are rounds as points, your stats are rounds as trends, the pot is rounds as stakes. Nothing else gets to be an atom.

2 · Nouns are places, verbs are sheets

Tabs answer questions; actions perform them. "Post a round" is not a place you visit, it's a thing you do — so it lives on the gold ⊕ and in context, never as a card squatting on a tab. Almost all of the pilot's "redundant" reports were verbs that had been promoted to places. Demote them and the clutter dissolves.

3 · One home

A home is where the app opens. By definition there is exactly one. Everything else is a room you visit and leave. The current app has three things that feel like home (league board, league-less welcome, the hub) which is why "two home tabs seemingly populate." In this architecture the home is the feed — full stop — and each league is a room inside Compete.

4 · Golf has a clock

Every golf interaction is before, during, or after a round. Plan a tee time. Play live (with side games). Post the score. Those are the same moment of life in three tenses, so they share one button. The ⊕ is not a "new post" button, it's an "I'm golfing" button.

5 · Show the work (spec §16)

Every number is a door. Standings row opens the counting rounds; a counting round opens its story; the story shows kudos, attestation, and the ledger entries it produced. This is already server-side law; the IA makes it navigation law. No dead-end numbers.

6 · The empty app is still an app

A golfer with zero leagues gets a complete product: a feed of their buddies' golf, their own card and stats, and the full ⊕. Competition is an upgrade, never a prerequisite. This kills the "what do I even do next" confusion and answers "game modes should be available from the get-go."

FOUR SLOTS · THE GOLD ⊕ IS A VERB, NOT A PLACE

Down from five tabs to four slots, and two of the five die entirely (Calendar and the People tab). Each slot is one question. The top bar of Home carries the two global utilities as overlays, the Instagram way: search (golfers, courses) and notifications (friend requests, league invites, kudos) — so social plumbing never needs a tab.

Naming: "Compete" is the clear choice; "Cups" is the on-brand alternative (every container mints a cup — seasons, Ryders, brackets). Decide by feel, the structure doesn't change.

05Object model

Five object families. Everything on screen is one of these or a view over one.

IDENTITY Golfer

Global. Name, @handle, marker, index, home course. Owns rounds and trophies; has buddies; joins containers. The card is its public face.

FACT Round

Immutable. Gross, rating/slope, date, index-at-post, differential. Belongs to a golfer, never to a league. Deletable only by its owner.

SOCIAL Story

What a fact looks like in public: a feed post carrying kudos, comments, attestation. Fans out to Home and every relevant league board.

ARENA Container

League (seasons → squads → standings → pot), Event (Ryder, Bracket, Major), or Live Round (a session with side games). Each is a lens over rounds, and each mints trophies.

LEDGER Adjustment

Floors, bonuses, voids, byes — every non-round point movement, with a reason. The "show the work" backbone; renders as system stories.

06User journeys

  1. The invited rookie (five minutes)

    Text link → email code → golfer card → lands on Home. Invite banner is the first thing on screen. Accept → League Room. First round posted from ⊕ that weekend; sees his own story on the feed and his name on the standings. Never saw an empty screen.

    link → sign in → card → Home banner → Accept → ⊕ → feed

  2. The Sunday post (ninety seconds, parking lot)

    ⊕ → Post a round → recent-course chip fills rating/slope → gross → done. The story hits the feed; two buddies kudos it; the league standings chip on Compete now reads "2nd · 4 back." He didn't visit a single page to make all that happen.

    ⊕ → chip → gross → post

  3. The small crew, short season

    Five guys, no appetite for squads. Compete → Start → League → Casual preset: uncapped individual race, no floors, three-week season starting Friday. Invites staged through the People Picker. Whole thing set up during one group-chat argument.

    Compete → Start → Casual → any-day start → invite → lock

  4. Saturday golf, organized to settled

    Tuesday: ⊕ → Plan a tee time → crew notified, it sits in Up Next all week. Saturday: ⊕ → Play now → Wolf with a guest. After: real rounds post through the same flow, the Wolf settle-up renders, nobody argues math.

    ⊕ plan → ⊕ play → ⊕ post

  5. The Pro at month-end

    Home's Up Next strip: "PIGL closes in 3 days." League Room → Standings shows who's short of the floor → nudge from the roster row. After the close, the ledger explains every floor hit as a system story on the Board. Zero DMs asking "why did we lose 10 points."

    Up Next → Room → Standings → nudge → ledger story

  6. The fat-fingered gross

    Posted 89, meant 98. You → Rounds → × on the round → confirm. Standings recompute everywhere because the views read facts. No support ticket, no Pro intervention.

    You → Rounds → × → done

09Migration path — five shippable phases

This is a live pilot, not a greenfield. Each phase is one deployable version that leaves the app better alone; order chosen so the biggest confusion (three homes) dies first.

P1

One Home. Merge league board(s) + buddy rounds into a single feed with the Up Next strip. Retire the quick-post card. The "two homes" and "friends' rounds feed" feedback die in the same build.

P2

League Room. Standings · Board · Schedule · Pot · Manage as segments behind one door in Compete. Calendar tab and pot page retire. "Start something" renders league-less.

P3

You. Public card with display case on top, stats and rounds archive below, settings behind the gear. The card becomes what a buddy sees when they tap your name anywhere.

P4

⊕ triptych. Post / Play now / Plan a tee time. Wolf and the side games return inside Play now. Plain-language scoring copy replaces the "SND" panel.

P5

Retire the People tab. Search overlay + notification bell + You · Buddies take over. Last because it's polish on a pattern the earlier phases prove.

Nothing wasted: the friends rework (v23.118–120) built the one component this whole architecture leans on — the People Picker. The XSS pass, the invite plumbing, the realtime spine, the flexible seasons: all of it slots in unchanged. This blueprint moves furniture, not foundations.
