4/11 · THE STRUCTURE

One number per league — about six bucks a man at any size

Three flat prices by roster size, but the app only ever quotes one — the wizard knows the crew. Flat for the Pro (one number to say out loud), per-head-fair for the crew (everyone pays about a fiver, any roster).

Crew sizeThe passPer playerOn a $75 buy-in pot
8–9 (small crew)$49$5.44–6.137–8% of the pot
10–13 (most leagues)$79$6.08–7.90~9%
14–16 (big crew)$99$6.19–7.07~8%

Band fixes at roster lock — adds or drops mid-season never reprice. Season 2 re-reads its own roster. A flat $79 for everyone was rejected because it charges the smallest, most price-sensitive crews double per head; a $6 per-head meter was rejected because it reads as a seat tax and reprices on every roster change.

THE FAIRNESS SENTENCE the Pro tells the crew — “The app's $79 for the season out of the pot — call it six bucks a man. It keeps the books, the standings, and the whole season; nobody touches a spreadsheet.”

9/11 · ROLLOUT

Rollout — honest at launch, paid at proven value

Mid-August 2026 · iOS launch Visible model, no checkout

Pricing public and honest on the wizard, You tab, and League Room — first-season-free leads everywhere. No payment rails; Stripe stays parked. App Store metadata: free to download, no in-app purchases, a league's first season free, season pass after. The app's front door stays pricing-free.

Aug–Sep 2026 · this deck's job The script closes the price point

15-minute sessions with PIGL (behavior only) and 3–5 arms-length Pros (the price signal). Slide 10 is the run-of-show; seat-3 medians pick the final number inside the $49/79/99 band frame.

First leagues hit season 2 · early 2027 Checkout ships — on the web

Stripe on cupseason.app; the Pro buys at a desk, the app shows pass status. ~$2.59 in fees on a $79 pass vs $11.85 at Apple's 15%. The run-it-back surface (slide 8) goes live. Founding Leagues never see it.

Standing posture · verified 2026-07-21 Apple: sell on the web, link out while allowed, IAP only as fallback

US storefront currently allows external purchase links, no entitlement, fee paused — but the regime is contested (Ninth Circuit narrowed the zero-fee bar Dec 2025; SCOTUS took the case June 2026). We build nothing that depends on link-out staying free: web sale with in-app unlock is commission-free under any outcome. Small Business Program (15%) enrolled as option value. Full memo: spec/pricing-discovery-2026-07.md §6.

10/11 · THE 15-MINUTE SCRIPT

Run of show — willingness to pay, 15 minutes

1:1, live. PIGL answers count for behavior, not price (they're friends and they're free forever). The 3–5 arms-length Pros carry the price decision.

0–2 minThe pot walk (deck hidden)
“Walk me through last season's money — buy-in, who collected, where it sat, what organizing cost you in dollars and hours.”
2–4 minShow the thing
Slide 2 (the model), then slide 3 with THEIR numbers in the dials. No price question yet.
4–8 minThe four fences (Van Westendorp)
Against your pot: so cheap you'd doubt it? · a bargain, easy yes? · getting expensive? · too expensive, period? Record all four numbers.
8–11 minThe gatekeeper pair — the study's most important data
Pro: “$79 for the season — who decides, pot or your pocket?” · Member (separately): “Your Pro says it's six bucks each — instant yes, grumble, or no?” The gap between answers is the conversion risk.
11–13 minThe renewal cliff
“First season free, season 2 is $79 from the pot — re-up without blinking, or does ‘wait, it was free’ start a fight?”
13–14 minRyder à la carte (Trip Guys only)
“One-weekend Ryder: ~$20 for the event, or free but only inside a $79 season?”
14–15 minThe kill question
“What would make you not use this even if it were free?”

$0-pot Pros get one extra probe: “No money league — the app's still $79. Split six ways, or does that kill it?” · Full instrument + scoring: spec/pricing-discovery-2026-07.md §5 and spec/focus-group-plan.md.
