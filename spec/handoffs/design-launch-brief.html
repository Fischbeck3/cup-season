Track A · Brand & design

The mark works. It hasn't met a designer yet.

It was built in-browser with canvas exports and hand-tuned SVG paths because there was no rasterizer on hand — functional, not final. The concept is worth keeping; the execution is worth an hour with someone who draws for a living.

Wordmark is set in IBM Plex Mono, weight 600, tracked ~0.32em, always caps. Interface type is IBM Plex Sans. Both carry over unchanged — this handoff is about the mark and the icon system, not the typeface.

What to bring to the designer

  • A micro-mark for small sizes. Four thin arcs hold up at app-icon size; they may not survive a 16px favicon or a notification badge. Worth a one- or two-arc reduction for anything under ~24px.
  • True depth, not path order. The violet arc reads as "behind the pole" only because it was drawn first. A designer can build that occlusion properly instead of relying on draw order.
  • Platform-native icon layers. Current icons are flat canvas exports. iOS rounds its own corners; Android adaptive icons want separate foreground/background layers. Neither has a proper source file yet.
  • The rest of the ball-marker set. Eight shape-named archetypes shipped (never real course references — that's a trademark problem waiting to happen); the roadmap calls for a full set of roughly twenty-four. This is illustrator work, not engineering work.
  • A colorblind pass. Green and orange — two of the four squad colors — are a classic confusion pair for deuteranopia. If squad color is ever someone's only cue, it needs a shape or pattern backup.
  • One motion moment. Nothing animates yet — not the splash, not a kickoff-post reveal. Even a short, simple sequence would go a long way toward "the app feels alive," which is a stated product principle, not a nice-to-have.

Bring back

A true vector master (not a canvas export), a platform-correct icon set for iOS/Android/PWA, and the ring formula documented well enough that engineering can regenerate every asset from it without re-inventing the upload pipeline.

Track B · Launch & growth

PIGL is the pitch. Everyone else just hasn't seen it yet.

Cup Season doesn't need an audience before launch — it needs one league finishing a season for real. That's what's happening right now. The plan below is about turning that into the next five leagues without spending on ads or building a marketing site first.

Who it's for, in their own words

The commissioner
"I'll run the league so you can enjoy it."
The weekend regular
"Every round counts."
The buddies-trip guy

The home-course golfer
Same faces, same course, season-long bragging rights.

The loop is already built in

A league isn't a feature that needs distribution — it's a distribution mechanic wearing a scoreboard. Every commissioner who starts one invites four to sixteen friends who didn't have Cup Season profiles yesterday. PIGL going first is the whole growth strategy in miniature: get one league to actually finish a season, then let its members be the ones who start the next one.

Where to spend attention, roughly in order

  1. Finish PIGL for real.Not the demo — the live season, with real money in the pot and real rounds posted. Every rough edge PIGL hits is a rough edge a stranger would have bounced off of.
  2. Let PIGL open the next door.No new tooling needed — every member already has an invite link and a code. The ask is just an ask: "start your own."
  3. Go where the leagues already are, badly.Golf-trip subreddits and Discords, home-course league organizers running things by spreadsheet and Venmo and a group text — the exact pain the commissioner promise is written for.
  4. Course-level partnerships, once there are two live leagues to point to.Municipal and semi-private courses that host informal leagues are a natural distribution partner — the community course-card seed strategy doubles as the pitch: add your course, invite your league.
  5. Show results, not features.A kickoff post, a Wolf-mode buzzer-beater, a Cup Final reveal — real moments from a real league beat a feature tour every time. The share card and email lockups are already built for this.

What the pitch sounds like

  • Every round counts.The season is the whole point — not another place to log a score that disappears.
  • The commissioner is off the hook.No more spreadsheet, no more Venmo chase, no more "wait, what were the rules again."
  • It's still your group chat — it just keeps score now.Real friends, real courses, nothing simulated.
