Architecture reference · Cup Season

Are we building this right?

How Cup Season's architecture stacks up against Strava, fantasy-football backends (Sleeper / ESPN), and team-collaboration apps (Discord / Slack) — what to keep, and where the real gap is.

The short answer: the backend is the right shape. The gap is the client.

Every one of these apps separates immutable, user-owned facts (an activity, a player's stat line, a message, a round) from the group-specific lenses that score and rank them (segments, league scoring, channels, league bylaws) — sitting on top of a global identity + social graph that cuts across every group. Cup Season's rounds store facts → leagues are lenses → profiles are global is that exact architecture. Sleeper migrated databases over getting this right; Strava rebuilt its leaderboards around it.

What's not validated is the client. There's no reusable component layer, which is why "add friends" ended up built six different ways. That's the real architectural debt — and the Crew / People-Picker work you just started is the first correct instance of fixing it.

The canonical shape

Strip away the scale differences and all four are the same three layers. Cup Season maps onto it cleanly.

1 · Global layer  ✓ we have this
Identity, the social graph, and immutable facts. One row per user, one per fact.  profiles · friendships · rounds · courses
↓
2 · Membership layer  ✓ we have this
A thin join that says who's in what, with roles. Powers the switcher, access checks, "which leagues do we share."  league_members · event_players
↓
3 · Tenant / lens layer  ✓ we have this
Per-group scoring, standings, and board — computed off the shared facts, never forking them.  v_rounds_ranked · v_squad_standings · posts

Friends live in layer 1 (global), which is why they must not be trapped inside any one tab or league — the exact bug the Crew rework fixes. Discord is the closest model: one account, an account-level friends graph, servers that merely reference users.

What to do about it

Keep
The facts → lenses split.rounds → v_rounds_ranked is the architecture Sleeper and Strava spent real pain to get right. Protect this boundary above all; never mutate a round.
Keep
Global identity + cross-cutting friends graph.Discord's exact model. The Crew / People-Picker work is finishing this — friends belong to the person, not a league.
Keep
Immutable rounds + adjustments ledger, and DB-enforced identity.Event-log-plus-reversible-ledger is the industry-standard correctness pattern, not idiosyncratic caution. Security-definer RPCs are the right version of commissioner tools.
Fix now
Establish a client component layer.The one real gap. No reusable components is why friends got built six times. Build a small shared library — a person-row, the People Picker, a board-row, a card — and reuse everywhere. Highest-leverage architectural investment you can make; you've started it.
Soon
Model unread + notifications properly.A per-(profile, board) last-seen marker, mentions as a distinct class, and Slack's "notable to X" vs "buzz their phone" split. Directly answers the pilot's "I don't know what's new or what to do next."
Soon
Make every recompute idempotent.Like close_month()'s month_closed sentinel — apply that everywhere a projection is rebuilt, so a re-run or backfill can never double-count.
Later
Precompute-on-write standings.On-read views are fine for ~12 golfers. When standings feel slow or leagues multiply, the upgrade is a standings_snapshots totals table written per round — not a fancier view. Don't build it early.
Later
Local-first sync & hybrid fan-out.Linear's local-first engine gives native-feel posting on patchy course-side connections; Strava's hybrid fan-out handles celebrity accounts. Both are real patterns, neither matters at friend-group scale. Know they exist; don't pay for them yet.
