# The Founding Pro Playbook — recruiting + the one-motion session

Growth/Launch lane · 2026-07-17 · the field manual for bible §11 step 2.

**The premise (argued in this lane, now operational):** four launch
workstreams are all blocked on the same missing input — arms-length golf
organizers. The timed QA needs uncoached testers, the pricing study needs
Seat 3, the Founding League program needs its panel, and Q1 GTM needs Pros to
white-glove. **One recruited Pro serves all four. This playbook extracts all
four from each conversation, in the right order.**

This doc *operationalizes* `spec/focus-group-plan.md` (which stays the
question canon — blocks A–E live there, not here) and `spec/prelaunch-qa-
2026-07-13.md` (which stays the QA script). It closes three of the plan's open
next-steps: recruiting messages, interviewer script, and the Seat-3 pipeline.

## 2. Where the arms-length people come from (in order of warmth)

1. **The PIGL second hop.** Each PIGL member names ONE person who organizes
   *another* group — a league, a skins game, an annual trip. Twelve members ≈
   8–10 names, all one introduction away and none of them Jerecho's friends.
   This is the single richest source; do it first, in the group chat, this week.
2. **Muni men's clubs** (Papago, ASU Karsten, Ken McDonald, Rolling Hills…):
   the men's-club board *is* a room full of Pros. One flyer-with-QR on the
   board + one conversation with the club president each.
3. **Local FB golf groups** (Phoenix/East Valley golf): post the honest-founder
   ask (message C below), DM organizers who comment.
4. **The starter's window.** Starters know exactly which regulars run games.
   Ask two starters for two names each.

r/golf is for *presence*, not recruiting (GTM §5) — locals only for now; the
sessions want a tee-box or a coffee table, and Founding Leagues want geography
we can white-glove.

## 3. The asks (send as-is, adjust names)

**A · The second-hop intro (PIGL group chat):**
> Crew — I need a favor that costs you one text. Who's ONE guy you know who
> runs his own game — a league, a skins group, a trip? Not to sell him
> anything: I need 5 organizers who'll give me an unfiltered hour before I
> price this thing. Intro me and I'll owe you a beer (on top of the ones I
> already owe you).

**B · The direct ask (Seat 3/4, via intro or DM):**
> Hey {name} — {mutual} said you run {their game}. I built an app for running
> golf seasons (my group's been on it all year) and before I put a price on
> it I need reads from people who actually run leagues — not my friends, and
> not a pitch. 45 minutes, coffee/beer on me: you poke at the app cold while
> I shut up and time you, then I ask you how your league's money actually
> works. Your league plays free for life if you end up wanting in, but that's
> not the ask. In?

**C · The FB-group version (public post):**
> Built a season app for my Tempe golf league (draft, handicaps, standings,
> the pot). Before I price it I want brutal feedback from people who RUN
> groups — leagues, skins games, trips. 45 min, beer's on me, no pitch, your
> crew plays free if you like it. DM me if that's you.

**D · The skeptic (Seat 6):**
> {name} — I heard you tried {app/spreadsheet} for your group and dropped it.
> Perfect. I built a golf-league app and I'd rather hear what killed that one
> from you than learn it from my own funeral. 30 minutes, beer on me?

The register throughout: founder-scratching-own-itch, "no pitch," money
honesty. Never "beta," never "startup," never "disrupt."

## 4. The session — 45 minutes, strict order (contamination is the enemy)

**Why this order:** the QA gates need COLD eyes (an uncoached first-timer),
and the pricing blocks need someone who has SEEN the product work. Running QA
first satisfies both — **the QA run doubles as the product demo the
focus-group plan requires before Block B.** Reversed, you burn the cold read
and get nothing back.

### Part A — the stopwatch (≈15 min, QA doc is the script)
- Their phone, the installed PWA (add to home screen first — that's part of
  gate 1's clock), a fresh `you+tN@gmail.com` alias.
- **Interviewer's only lines:** "Get yourself set up like you'd actually use
  it. Talk out loud. I can't answer questions — if you're stuck, that's my
  bug, not yours."
- Run gates 1→3 on their fresh account (profile · join via a real invite link
  · post a round — hole-by-hole first, note if they reach for the escape
  hatch). Gate 4 (the 10-second standings read) runs on the demo league —
  it's populated and built for exactly this. Gate 5 = every "what do I do
  here?" moment, logged verbatim with its screen.
- Stopwatch every gate. Hesitation is a finding. **Log into the QA doc's
  findings log same-day** (date · gate · what stalled · fix).

### Part B — the money (≈25 min, focus-group plan is the script)
Transition line: *"Okay — now forget my app for a minute. Walk me through
last season's money in YOUR group."*
- Block A (pot anchor) → Block B (Van Westendorp + **the q8/q9 gatekeeper
  pair — the most important five minutes of the session**) → Block C
  (season-1-free) → Block D (Ryder, if Seat 4) → Block E (kill questions).
- PIGL seats skip Block B entirely (insider price reads are noise).

### Part C — the close (≈5 min, only if they lit up)
> "I'm taking ten leagues ever as Founding Leagues — free for life, badge on
> the league, direct line to me — in exchange for actually playing a season
> and answering my questions once a month. Want one of the ten?"
- Say the soft obligation OUT LOUD (the plan requires it).
- If yes: schedule the white-glove call before you leave the table (GTM §12
  — it's the highest-leverage hour in the company). Hand them a coin when
  the minted markers exist (mark brief).
- If lukewarm: no pitch, thank them, leave the claim-link door open — invite
  them to the next PIGL skins game instead (the live-game wedge, GTM §13.2).

## 5. The synthesis grid (fill after every session)

One row per person, kept in this file's log below:
`name/seat · QA times (G1–G4) · zero-tutorial misses · pot size · current
spend ($ + hrs) · VW four prices · q8/q9 gap (who decides / would members
chip in) · S1-free reaction · Ryder read · kill answer · Founding? Y/N/maybe`

**Decision thresholds (pre-committed so the data can't be argued with later):**
- Pricing recommendation drafts after **5 completed Seat-3/4 grids**; it goes
  to `decision-log.md` per protocol.
- If the q8/q9 gap shows members-would-pay-but-Pros-balk in 3+ grids, the
  pot-line-item mechanic (plan's "likely fix") gets designed before Stripe
  un-parks.
- Any QA gate missing its target twice → that friction fix jumps the task
  queue (a miss is a fix, not a debate — QA doc's own rule).

## 6. Cadence & accounting

- **Two sessions/week × 5 weeks** lands all seats inside the 90-day plan's
  months 1–2 (bible §7) with the pricing decision due in month 3.
- Cost: ~10 beers/coffees + ~15 minted coins. That's the entire budget, and
  it's also the entire Q1 acquisition, QA, pricing, and panel program.
- Founding cap: PIGL is №1; close the wall loudly at ~8–10 (scarcity is the
  artifact — focus-group plan §Founding).
